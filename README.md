# prusa-mini-corexy-parts
Additional parts for building the Prusa Mini CoreXY by SE from Printables.

The parts that you can find in this repository were designed and used by myself to build the awesome Prusa Mini CoreXY by SE from Printables. 

You can find the project here on Printables: https://www.printables.com/model/681983-prusa-mini-corexy

Make sure to check the original parts, as well as the comment section, where some people just revealed their builds and additional parts first and maybe do some measurements as well in the slicer and check the parts for errors. Maybe I've mistaken something...

For me, some parts like the X carriage shroud caused problems in the slicer, cause the walls were apparently to thin, some were to weak... Doesn't neccessarily mean, it's the same for you.

In case you have the same problems like I do, the parts attached in this repo replace the originally from SE provided parts for building the printer - those are the parts I've used for building my printer, which you can see in this video: 

You can find all the parts in the 'assets' - folder.

There is a folder called 'SpacerShims' in the 'assets' - folder. You can find various spacers and shims there. They are being used to position and hold the idlers in the Y carriages. Honestly, I forgot, which ones I've used. Just print all of them, its really just a few grams and see, what fits best for you. Maybe in the future I will do an assembly video, then I can definitely tell you, which ones I've used and in general, how the whole printer is being assembled, since there are no instructions at all. 

If I see some people interested in this project, I will do an assembly video for a basic / minimal but functional setup for a low budget of the printer.

Parts I've created / repaired / optimized are:

- X carriage (don't remember exactly what I've changed - I think I just made the middle part more stable to not crack under the pulling forces of the belts)

- fan mount for toolhead (so the fan is mounted more stable)

- X carriage shroud (original one's walls were too thin to be printed)

- front idler right (made it more rigid)

I also created some 'reinforcements' for the printer to stabilize the frame. You can see it in the video. Those parts are neccessary for one of these reinforcements:

- tower part up
- tower part down
- DiffPlate

The way the reinforcements work:

You can simply print the upper and lower part and one diff plate. The 'DiffPlate' will be layed between the upper and lower part an can be adjusted in thickness by scaling it up and down. You will want to print it in a few thicknesses to check out how thick the plate needs to be for your printer. It may vary a little bit to mine, but mine was 1.4 mm thick. By adjusting the thickness you can get the optimal support in strength for your frame. 

My recommended setup for you - get yourself these parts:

- 1x 3030 extrusion 300 mm (bottom front horizontal)
- 3x 3030 extrusion 240 mm (bottom sides horizontal and one upper back horizontal)
- 2x 3030 extrusion 285 mm (bottom front vertical)
- 2x 3030 extrusion 320 mm (bottom back vertical)

- 8 idlers GT2 20 teeth 5mm bore (no teeth) for 6mm belt

- 2x around 1100mm GT2 belt 6mm (maybe just get 5m, in case you mess up some measurements and I would strongly recommend quality ones with fibreglass)

- 100 pcs of M3 x 8mm cylinder screws

You will also need to drill a 5mm hole and cut a m6 thread with a tap in the Long 320mm 3030 extrusions to mount the motors left and Right on top. At least that's how I did it and it works.

All the other parts (screws, pulleys, motors, rods, fans...) are being reused from your Prusa Mini / Mini +, so disassemble it carefully. Read trhough the project on Printables carefully. Maybe a few times. Look at all the parts in the project. I can recommend to print a lot of the 'ALU 90' ninety degree connector parts to connect every 3030 extrusion from every side you can with the other 3030 extrusions, to make the frame really rigid. It will be! You can use the 'ALU nut M3' for it as well, to secure them on the 3030 extrusions. They work fine. Make sure to maybe scale them a little bit smaller, because they can be quite hard to be inserted into the extrusions otherwise - just print one in 100 percent scale and check, if adjustments are needed for you.

Feel free to ask questions. I will try to answer them - either here, or on Printables. On Printables you will probably get more support, because it's the projects origin :)
